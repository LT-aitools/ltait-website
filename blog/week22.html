Week 22

From Manual to Automated - Streamlining Our Blog Creation Process

Published on September 9, 2025

This blog post was written and published by a team of agents under Claude Code, based on a transcript of a meeting between two humans, Netta and Charlie.

• • •

This week was all about solving a problem that's been nagging us for months: our blog creation process was painfully manual and time-consuming. Charlie dove deep into automating everything from transcript processing to screenshot selection, with mixed but promising results.

Blog Process Time Analysis

The Problem

We finally did what we should have done months ago - actually timed how long our blog creation process takes. Charlie broke down every single step and the results were eye-opening:

  • Initial setup: 15 minutes (downloading meeting video, running transcripts, getting Granola notes, updating Claude projects)
  • Blog writing and editing: 40 minutes (running blog post generation, manual editing, timestamp checking)
  • Final processing: Additional time for social media, Medium posting, etc.

By the time we even got to writing the actual blog post, we'd already spent 15 minutes just on prep work. The manual editing was particularly painful - copying, pasting, and fixing various issues like attribution problems, adding needed context, and replacing screenshots/videos.

While this analysis was necessary foundational work, it mainly confirmed what we already suspected: our process was inefficient and needed major improvements.

• • •

Granola Direct Blog Writing

The Experiment

Since Granola already had our meeting transcripts and its own AI-generated notes, we wondered: could it just write our blog posts directly? We asked it to generate a blog post from scratch without our usual elaborate multi-step process.

Results

The output was decent but fell short in several key areas:

  • Too short: Lacked the detailed explanations our readers expect
  • Format issues: Didn't match our established blog structure
  • Surface level: When we asked for more detail, it made every section the same length, turning brief mentions into weirdly expanded sections

The bigger problem was timestamp accuracy. When we asked Granola to suggest timestamps for screenshots and video clips, it was completely wrong. It seemed to think our 100-minute meeting was only an hour long and never suggested timestamps beyond the 60-minute mark.

Grade: C-

Granola showed promise for basic blog generation but failed completely at the visual content selection that makes our posts engaging.

• • •

Claude Direct Blog Writing

The Process

Instead of our usual elaborate prompt chain, we gave Claude just a simple instruction and the raw transcript. The results surprised us - the writing quality was actually better than our original process.

We started with a minimal prompt and iteratively refined it:

  1. First attempt: Good content but too many bullet points, felt like meeting notes
  2. Second attempt: Asked for narrative prose, but it overcorrected and became too wordy
  3. Third attempt: Found the sweet spot with "balance between bullet points and narrative prose"

Key improvements we added:

  • Write in first person using "we"
  • Skip administrative discussion at the start of meetings
  • Don't artificially assign projects to individuals when we work collaboratively

Results

The final output was significantly better than our manual process:

  • More natural writing flow
  • Better section organization that adapted to each project's needs
  • Maintained consistency while allowing flexibility
  • Much faster generation time
Grade: A-

This approach dramatically improved both quality and efficiency. The writing felt more natural and engaging than our previous multi-step process.

• • •

Screenshot Timestamp Automation Attempts

The Challenge

The most time-consuming part of our process is finding good screenshots and video clips. We spend 15+ minutes manually scrubbing through recordings to find relevant moments. Could we automate this?

The Failures

Both Claude and Granola failed spectacularly at this task:

Granola's problems:

  • Thought our 100-minute meeting was only 60 minutes long
  • Never suggested any timestamps after the one-hour mark
  • When asked for 10-minute breakdowns, it just assigned each topic exactly 10 minutes regardless of actual discussion length

Claude's problems:

  • Opposite issue - thought our meeting was 2 hours 40 minutes when it was only 1 hour 40 minutes
  • Same artificial time allocation problem as Granola
  • Screenshots it selected were often from irrelevant moments (like "can you see my screen?" instead of actual demos)

One Small Win

When we flipped the approach and asked Claude to find timestamps for specific topics (rather than describing what happened at each timestamp), it got reasonably close - within 30 seconds of the right moments for most topics.

Grade: D+

While we found one approach that worked somewhat, the screenshot automation remains our biggest unsolved challenge.

• • •

Claude Code Blog Agent Setup

The Vision

Inspired by our successes with direct Claude writing, we decided to build a comprehensive automation system using Claude Code. The goal: fully automate everything from downloading meeting videos to publishing finished blog posts.

The Implementation

We set up a sophisticated system with:

  • Documentation standards: PRD docs, progress tracking, session summaries
  • Quality control loops: Content managers and project managers reviewing work
  • Multi-agent workflow: Specialized sub-agents for different aspects
  • Pre-commit hooks: Automated testing and quality checks

The system includes roles for content writers, project managers, and quality reviewers, each with specific expertise and responsibilities.

Early Results

The initial test showed both promise and remaining challenges:

  • Blog writing: Decent quality, though not quite as good as our manual Claude prompting
  • Screenshot selection: Still problematic - only about 3 out of 15 images/videos would have been acceptable
  • Process complexity: The full automation uses significant Claude Code credits (we hit usage limits during our testing process)
Grade: B

Ambitious and well-structured, but still needs refinement. The infrastructure is solid, but the output quality needs improvement to match our manual processes.

• • •

Looking Ahead

This week highlighted both the promise and challenges of automation. While we made significant progress on content generation, visual content selection remains our biggest hurdle. The Claude direct writing approach is immediately usable and dramatically improves our efficiency. We suspect Anthropic's model improvements since we created our original workflow has allowed for this.

Our next steps:

  • Refine the Claude Code agent system based on this week's learnings
  • Explore alternative approaches for screenshot and video clip selection
  • Test notebook LM for one-shot blog generation
  • Integrate social media automation once the core blog process is solid

The goal isn't just to save time - it's to maintain or improve quality while removing the tedious manual work that prevents us from experimenting with more AI tools each week.
